<p><p>The weed control services market is segmented into warm-season and cool-season weeds. Warm-season weeds thrive in the heat, typically emerging in late spring and causing issues during summer. They often dominate in southern regions. In contrast, cool-season weeds flourish in cooler temperatures, appearing in early spring or fall, primarily affecting northern and transitional areas. Effective weed management strategies vary based on these two types, as each requires tailored approaches to maintain healthy landscapes and ensure optimal plant growth.</p></p>
