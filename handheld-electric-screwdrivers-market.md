<p><p>The handheld electric screwdriver market is primarily divided into two types: AC and DC. AC screwdrivers are powered by alternating current, often providing consistent torque and are suitable for heavy-duty applications in workshops or industrial settings. In contrast, DC screwdrivers run on direct current, offering portability and ease of use, making them ideal for residential and light-duty tasks. Each type caters to different user needs, with AC tools focusing on power and performance, while DC models emphasize convenience and flexibility.</p></p>
