<p><p>The automotive Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC) system market consists of three primary technologies: Radar, LIDAR, and Sensor Fusion. Radar systems use radio waves to detect vehicle speed and distance, offering reliability in various weather conditions. LIDAR employs laser beams for high-precision mapping and object detection, providing detailed spatial awareness. Sensor Fusion combines data from multiple sensors, enhancing overall system accuracy and performance. Together, these technologies contribute to the development of advanced driver-assistance systems, improving safety and driving efficiency.</p></p>
