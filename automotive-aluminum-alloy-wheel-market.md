<p><p>The automotive aluminum alloy wheel market primarily consists of three types: casting, forging, and other methods. Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into molds, creating complex shapes with ease but often resulting in higher weight. Forging entails shaping aluminum under high pressure, producing stronger and lighter wheels. Other methods may include processes like extrusion or hybrid approaches. Each type offers distinct advantages in terms of performance, weight, and cost, catering to different automotive applications and consumer preferences in the market.</p></p>
