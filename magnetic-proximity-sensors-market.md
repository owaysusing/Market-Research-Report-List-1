<p><p>Magnetic proximity sensors are essential for detecting the presence or absence of objects without physical contact, primarily used in automation and safety applications. Inductive sensors utilize electromagnetic fields to sense metallic objects, while capacitance sensors detect changes in capacitance caused by nearby materials, including non-metallic ones. Photoelectric sensors use light beams to determine object distance, while magnetic sensors respond to changes in magnetic fields, making them effective for detecting magnetic materials. Each type serves unique applications in industrial settings.</p></p>
