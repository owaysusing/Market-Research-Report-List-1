<p><p>Implantable Collamer Lens (ICL) surgery is a vision correction procedure that involves the surgical implantation of a lens inside the eye, positioned between the iris and the natural lens. This option is particularly popular among individuals with moderate to severe myopia (nearsightedness) and those who may not be suitable candidates for laser refractive surgery. ICL offers advantages such as reversible correction, minimal impact on the corneal structure, and excellent visual outcomes.</p><p>The ICL surgery market is expected to experience significant growth, driven by factors such as increasing prevalence of refractive errors, advancements in lens technology, and a rise in awareness regarding vision correction options.
