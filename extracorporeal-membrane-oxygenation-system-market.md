<p><p>The Extracorporeal Membrane Oxygenation (ECMO) System is a life-support technology used to provide prolonged cardiac and respiratory support to patients whose heart and lungs are severely compromised. It circulates blood outside the body, through an artificial lung (membrane) that adds oxygen and removes carbon dioxide, before returning it to the patient. This system is crucial in critical care settings, especially for patients with conditions like severe pneumonia, cardiac failure, or during complex surgeries.</p><p>The ECMO System Market is witnessing significant growth driven by the rising incidence of respiratory and cardiac diseases, advancements in technology, and increasing awareness about emergency medical services. <p><ul><li>Veno-Arterial (VA)</li><li>Veno-Venous (VV)</li><li>Arterio-Venous (AV)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Extracorporeal Membrane Oxygenation (ECMO) system market consists of three main types: Veno-Arterial (VA), Veno-Venous (VV), and Arterio-Venous (AV). VA ECMO supports patients with both cardiac and respiratory failure by providing oxygenated blood to the body. VV ECMO is primarily used for severe respiratory issues, facilitating oxygenation while allowing the heart to function. AV ECMO is typically employed in patients undergoing treatments like dialysis, where blood is oxygenated and filtered simultaneously. Each type addresses specific clinical needs, enhancing patient outcomes.</p></p>
