<p><p>The Omega-3 polyunsaturated fatty acid market encompasses three primary types: Docosahexaenoic Acid (DHA), Eicosapentaenoic Acid (EPA), and Alpha-Linolenic Acid (ALA). DHA is crucial for brain health and is commonly found in fish oil, making it popular in supplements. EPA is known for its cardiovascular benefits and is often used in functional foods. ALA, a plant-based fatty acid, is found in flaxseeds and walnuts, catering to vegetarians and vegans. Each type serves unique health benefits, influencing market demand.</p></p>
